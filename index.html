The problem

Schools have robotics equipment. What many lack is a clear way forward.

Schools have invested in robotics kits, coding tools, competitions, and STEM programs. Yet many programs remain fragmented, dependent on one teacher, disconnected from curriculum, or uncertain about how robotics should evolve as AI transforms the field.

An uncertain future

Schools are unsure how robotics, AI, automation, and autonomous systems should connect across grades and subjects.

More technology does not automatically create a stronger program.

Schools need to understand what they already have, what is missing, and what should come next.

Why now

Robotics is changing—and schools need help deciding what remains educationally valuable.

As robotics becomes more connected to AI, sensing, automation, and adaptive behaviour, schools face a more complicated question than which kit to purchase.

“AI teaches students how machines make decisions. Robotics shows what happens when those decisions become actions in the physical world.”

The transition challenge

02

Connect robotics learning with AI and autonomous systems.
